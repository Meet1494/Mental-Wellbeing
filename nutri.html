Leafy Greens

Leafy greens such as spinach, kale, and Swiss chard are high in magnesium, which plays a role in regulating neurotransmitters and promoting relaxation. Magnesium deficiency has been linked to increased anxiety, so including magnesium-rich foods in your diet may help manage symptoms.

Berries

Berries like blueberries, strawberries, and blackberries are packed with antioxidants, which can help combat stress and inflammation in the body. They also contain vitamin C, which has been shown to reduce stress hormone levels.

Nuts and Seeds

Nuts and seeds like almonds, walnuts, and sunflower seeds are rich in healthy fats, protein, and magnesium. They can help stabilize blood sugar levels and promote a sense of calmness.

Dark Chocolate

Dark chocolate with high cocoa content (70% or higher) contains flavonoids that have been shown to reduce stress hormones and improve mood. However, it's essential to consume dark chocolate in moderation due to its calorie and sugar content.
